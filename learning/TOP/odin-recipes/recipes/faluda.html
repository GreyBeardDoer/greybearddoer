Faluda - A Refreshinng Indian Dessert

Description

Faluda is a combination of ice-creams, fruits and sugar syrups. It is typically consumed during the scorching Indian summers. Along with Roohafza, it is also used as a refreshing iftaar drink during the holy Ramadan month.

Ingradients

  • Ice Cubes
  • Cold Water
  • Faluda Noodles
  • Chilled Full Fat Milk
  • Basil seeds
  • Ice Creams
  • Fruits
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Step 1: Boil the Faluda noodles untill they become soft and smooth.
  2. Step 2: Soak the Basil seeds in cold water till they become gelatinous.
  3. Step 3: Cut the fruits in very small pieces.
  4. Step 4: Create sugar syrup by mixing water and sugar.
  5. Step 5: Add the sugar syrup, soft boiled Faluda noodles, soaked basil seeds in a tall glass.
  6. Step 6: Add chilled full fat milk according to the thickness that you want.
  7. Step 7: Stir with a spoon till all the ingradients get mixed very well.
  8. Step 8: Now add the fruits and stir again to mix them well.
  9. Step 9: Finally sccop out a large dollop of ice cream and add it over the top of the mixture. Deliciious Faluda is ready. Serve it cold!
